BE MY MUSE — KoGPT-2-based Emotion-Driven Lyric AI

Contents

    Problem

    Competition background

    MUSE Label's "BE MY MUSE" competition asked entrants to listen to a piece of composed music and submit lyrics that fit it. The piece was an emotionally rich ballad. Our team's idea was simple: don't write the lyrics ourselves — let an AI do it.

    Technical challenge

    KoGPT-2 is strong on Korean, but it was trained on news, novels, and reports — formal, written-language text. That makes it ill-suited to ballad lyrics. Fine-tuning was required to teach the model to produce emotionally evocative lines.

    Solution

    Core concept

    Fine-tune KoGPT-2 on emotion-rich lyrics so that, given just three keywords, it generates a ballad-flavored lyric.

    Key features

    • A Korean lyric dataset of 7,439 songs from the Melon chart
    • An emotion-driven lyric model produced by fine-tuning KoGPT-2
    • BLEU, ROUGE, and Perplexity-based generation-quality evaluation
    • Sentiment validation across 43 emotion categories (KOTE)

    Highlights

    • Training data — 7,439 songs collected → 4,840-song cleaned dataset
    • Fine-tuning — solo full fine-tuning of KoGPT-2 (125M)
    • Sentiment validation — quality check across 43 KOTE emotion categories
    • Multi-metric evaluation — BLEU + ROUGE + Perplexity quantitative pipeline
    • Competition — submitted to MUSE Label's "BE MY MUSE"

    What I Built

    1. Data collection

    Of the 7,439 songs collected, I owned the hip-hop subset.
    Lyrics were scraped from the Melon chart with Selenium-based dynamic crawling.

    2. Model fine-tuning

    • Set up a 2× RTX 4090 training environment
    • Solo-ran the entire KoGPT-2 (skt/kogpt2-base-v2) fine-tuning pipeline
    • Tuned generation parameters (temperature, top_k, top_p)
    • Hyperparameter sweep over learning rate, batch size, epochs
    • Determined the optimal epoch count from results to avoid overfitting

    3. Evaluation

    • 3 keywords in → generated lyric out, evaluated
    • BLEU: n-gram accuracy of generated text
    • ROUGE: overlap with reference text
    • Perplexity: model confidence and naturalness

    Data Pipeline

    • Collection: Selenium dynamic crawling of the Melon chart (7,439 songs)
    • Cleaning: dedup, normalization, tokenization → 4,840-song training set
    • Tokenizer: KoGPT-2 tokenizer with added special tokens
    • Training format: [BOS] keywords: {keyword} lyrics: {lyrics} [EOS]

    Model Architecture

    Fine-tuning settings

    • Base Model: SKT KoGPT-2-base-v2 (125M parameters)
    • GPU: RTX-4090 (24 GB VRAM)
    • Hyperparameters: lr=5e-5, batch_size=8, epochs=10
    • Optimizer: AdamW with weight decay

    Generation-parameter tuning

    • temperature: 0.8 (creativity / coherence balance)
    • top_k: 50, top_p: 0.95 (diversity)
    • repetition_penalty: 1.2 (anti-repetition)

    Metrics & Evaluation

    Quantitative metrics

    Standard NLP baselines
    • BLEU: 0–1 range, ≥ 0.3 considered good (translation tasks)
    • ROUGE: 0–1 range, higher is more similar to the reference (summarization tasks)
    • Perplexity: GPT-2 benchmark roughly 16–20, lower is more natural

    Interpretation for BeMyMuse
    The competition is about creativity. Very high BLEU/ROUGE would mean the generated lyrics resemble existing ones too closely — a plagiarism risk.
    So we treat moderately low BLEU/ROUGE + low Perplexity as the marker of "creative yet natural" lyrics.

    Sentiment validation

    We classified generated lyrics into 43 emotion categories with KOTE (Korean Online That Evaluation).

    If emotions appropriate to ballads (sadness, longing, love, loneliness, etc.) are dominant, the fine-tuned model is producing the intended emotional content.

    Outcomes & Retrospective

    Key outcomes

    • Built a 4,840-song cleaned lyric dataset
    • Fine-tuned KoGPT-2 to generate natural Korean lyrics
    • Shipped a polished service: FastAPI + Streamlit UI
    • Demonstrated the value of AI as a creative-writing aid via sentiment analysis and contextual visualization

    Lessons learned

    Initially we tried random combinations from KoNLPy + the National Institute of the Korean Language corpus. Korean's complex grammar — particles and conjugation — made simple recombination produce unnatural sentences.
    That phase was painful. Insufficient deep-learning know-how, rough engineering practice, and an underestimation of Korean's particularities — partly because Korean is my mother tongue and many of its quirks were invisible to me.

    As I kept studying generative AI I became convinced that LLMs can, in fact, learn Korean's complex grammar. That shifted me to KoGPT-2 fine-tuning. Perplexity is still on the high side — there is room — but watching the model gradually learn Korean grammar and emotional expression was deeply rewarding and raised my ambition.

    Technical growth

    Through this project I learned the difference between Pre-training and Post-training (Fine-tuning) and the concept of SFT (Supervised Fine-Tuning). The biggest takeaway was understanding that an LLM is not "intelligence" but a probability-driven token generator.
    That reframing made me much more honest about what's possible and what isn't. I also learned that Korean is still tokenized inefficiently relative to English-centric models, so Korean models need more data and training. That has become a long-term motivation for me to keep building Korean NLP.
